# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud has become very common in India, especially among students and people who regularly use apps like Google Pay or PhonePe. This type of cybercrime usually happens when a fraudster tricks someone into sending money by mistake.

For example, a college student receives a call from someone pretending to be a delivery agent. The caller says a package cannot be delivered unless a small “verification payment” is made. The student is then asked to scan a QR code or approve a payment request. Thinking they are receiving money or completing a small payment, the student approves it—but instead, money gets deducted from their account.

In many cases, scammers also send fake links or messages that look like they are from banks or official apps. Once the victim enters their details or OTP, the scammer quickly transfers money.

People who are new to digital payments or not fully aware of how UPI works are the most targeted. The result is not just financial loss but also stress and regret.

This shows that such frauds are less about technology and more about manipulation. Staying alert and understanding how payments actually work can prevent these situations.
